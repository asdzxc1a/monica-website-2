# Screen Three Analysis

## Why the image dominates

`screen 3.png` is strong because it behaves less like a portrait and more like a stage set. One figure becomes three through mirror repetition, creating a closed triangular composition that feels ceremonial, secretive, and self-contained. The eye lands on the bright vertical of the spine and neck first, then moves outward to the mirrored profiles, then back down into the dark volume of the dress.

## Formal structure

- The composition is almost perfectly axial: the center figure holds the page together while the two reflections act like supporting columns.
- The top third is dense with hair texture and overlapping bun shapes, which creates a crown-like silhouette.
- The middle third is the emotional anchor: pale skin against black fabric gives the image its strongest contrast.
- The lower third falls into near-black, which makes the dress read as atmosphere rather than garment detail.

## Tonal behavior

- The image is monochrome, but not flat. It uses a long gray scale from silver highlights in the hair to deep charcoal in the dress and background.
- Highlights are concentrated at the upper back, nape, and ears, so the body reads as sculptural.
- The reflections are softer and dimmer than the center figure, which preserves hierarchy even though the subject is repeated.

## Emotional reading

- It feels intimate, introspective, and slightly theatrical.
- The mirrored faces turned inward suggest privacy rather than performance.
- Because the figure never faces the camera, the image feels editorial and art-directed rather than candid.

## Implications for the website rebuild

- This image should occupy the central, largest card because it provides the clearest focal point and strongest sense of authorship.
- Supporting images should act as narrative chapters around it: arrival, city exit, jewelry detail, fitting, and lighter candid moments.
- The page should stay dark and restrained so the black-and-white hero image remains the emotional center.
- Typography should be elegant, serif-led, and spacious, echoing the image’s classical symmetry rather than competing with it.
